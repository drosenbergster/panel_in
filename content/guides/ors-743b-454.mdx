# ORS 743B.454: Your Credentialing Rights

Oregon Revised Statute 743B.454 protects providers during the credentialing process. Understanding these rights is important — they give you leverage if a CCO delays your application and may allow you to bill during the credentialing period.

## What the Law Requires

ORS 743B.454 mandates that insurers (including CCOs) must:

1. **Complete credentialing within 90 days** of receiving a complete application
2. **Allow billing during credentialing** under certain conditions
3. **Pay at the non-participating rate** for services rendered during the credentialing period

## What Constitutes a "Complete Application"

A complete application means all required documents have been submitted and the application is not missing any information the CCO needs to begin processing. This typically includes:

- Completed credentialing form (OPCA, OHP 3108, or CCO-specific form)
- All required supporting documents (license, insurance, NPI verification)
- Any CCO-specific requirements (Seclusion & Restraint letter, Hospital Admit Plan, etc.)

**Tip:** When you submit, request written confirmation that your application is complete. This starts the 90-day clock.

## Billing During Credentialing

If your application is complete and the CCO has not finished credentialing within a reasonable timeframe, ORS 743B.454 allows you to:

- Begin seeing patients covered by that CCO
- Submit claims for services rendered
- Be paid at the **non-participating provider rate**

Once credentialing is complete, you transition to the contracted rate.

## 6-Month Claims Submission Window

You have **6 months from the date of service** to submit claims for services rendered during the credentialing period. Keep thorough records of:

- Date each service was provided
- Patient information and coverage verification
- The date you submitted your complete application

## The CareOregon Policy Nuance

**Important exception:** As of October 2025, CareOregon (covering AllCare, Jackson Care Connect, and Columbia Pacific) has implemented a policy requiring full credentialing **before** seeing members. This means the ORS 743B.454 billing-during-credentialing provision may not apply in practice for CareOregon-affiliated CCOs, because CareOregon will not accept claims from non-contracted providers.

If you believe a CareOregon-affiliated CCO is not processing your application within 90 days, contact the Oregon Division of Financial Regulation for guidance.

## How to Use This Information

1. **Track your submission date** — note when you sent your complete application
2. **Request confirmation** — ask the CCO to confirm receipt and completeness
3. **Mark the 90-day deadline** — if credentialing isn't complete by then, you have options
4. **Keep records** — document everything in case you need to escalate
5. **Know the exception** — CareOregon's non-contracted provider policy limits billing during credentialing

## If a CCO Exceeds 90 Days

If credentialing takes longer than 90 days from your complete application:

- Contact the CCO's provider services department
- Reference ORS 743B.454 and your submission date
- If unresolved, file a complaint with the [Oregon Division of Financial Regulation](https://dfr.oregon.gov/consumers/file-complaint)
