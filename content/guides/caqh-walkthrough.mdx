# CAQH ProView Walkthrough

CAQH ProView is a universal credentialing database used by most CCOs and payers. Completing your profile once lets multiple organizations pull your credentials without separate submissions. Some Oregon CCOs (notably Trillium/Centene) require CAQH authorization.

## Before You Start

Have these ready:

- NPI number (Type 1, individual)
- Oregon license number and expiration date
- Professional liability insurance face sheet
- Practice address(es)
- Work history for the last 5 years (all positions, mark current ones as "Current")
- Education history (graduate degree details)
- References (at least 3 — professional contacts who can verify your clinical competence)

## Step-by-Step Setup

### 1. Create Your Account

Go to [CAQH ProView](https://proview.caqh.org/) and register. You'll need your NPI. If a payer has already initiated your profile, you'll receive a CAQH Provider ID by email — use that to claim your existing profile.

### 2. Personal Information

Enter your legal name (must match your NPI and Oregon license exactly), date of birth, SSN, and contact information.

### 3. Professional IDs

Add your NPI (Type 1), Oregon LCSW license number, DEA number (if applicable — most LCSWs will select N/A), and any other state licenses.

### 4. Education & Training

Enter your graduate degree details: school name, degree type (MSW for LCSWs), dates attended, and graduation date.

### 5. Work History

List all professional positions for the past 5 years. Mark any current positions as "Current." Gaps longer than 6 months require an explanation.

### 6. Practice Locations

Add every address where you provide services. **Mobile practitioners**: list your primary business address. You can add notes about mobile/itinerant service delivery.

### 7. Hospital Affiliations

If you have hospital privileges, list them. Most outpatient-only therapists will select "No hospital affiliations."

### 8. Professional Liability Insurance

Upload your current insurance face sheet. Ensure it shows: your name, policy number, coverage dates, and limits (minimum $1M/$3M recommended).

### 9. Malpractice History

Disclose any malpractice claims or settlements. Most new practitioners will have none.

### 10. Disclosure Questions

Answer health, legal, and professional conduct questions honestly. These are standard credentialing disclosures.

### 11. References

Add at least 3 professional references. These should be licensed professionals who can speak to your clinical competence. They do not need to be from the same organization.

### 12. Upload Supporting Documents

Upload: Oregon license copy, liability insurance face sheet, W-9, and any other required documents as PDFs.

### 13. Authorize Payers

This is critical for Oregon CCOs. Go to the "Manage Authorizations" section and authorize these organizations to access your profile:

| Organization | For Which CCO |
|-------------|---------------|
| Centene Corporation | Trillium Community Health Plan |
| Moda Health | EOCCO |
| CareOregon | AllCare, Jackson Care Connect, Columbia Pacific |
| PacificSource | PacificSource Gorge, Marion/Polk, Central Oregon |

### 14. Attest and Submit

Review all entries, then click "Attest." Your attestation is valid for **120 days**. Set a calendar reminder to re-attest before it expires — expired attestations block credentialing.

## After Submission

Your CAQH profile is now visible to authorized payers. They will pull your information during their credentialing process. Keep your profile updated — any changes (new address, insurance renewal, license renewal) should be reflected promptly.
