# Malpractice Insurance

Professional liability (malpractice) insurance is required by every Oregon CCO and Medicare. Getting the right coverage before you start any application avoids delays.

## Recommended Coverage

The safe universal standard that satisfies all Oregon CCOs:

| Coverage | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| **Per occurrence** | $1,000,000 minimum |
| **Aggregate** | $3,000,000 minimum |

<TimeEstimate minutes={15} />

Umpqua Health Alliance explicitly requires $1M/$3M. Other CCOs may accept lower limits, but carrying $1M/$3M means you never have to check — it satisfies everyone.

## Per-CCO Minimums

| CCO | Documented Minimum |
|-----|-------------------|
| Umpqua Health Alliance | $1M per occurrence / $3M aggregate (explicit) |
| All CareOregon-affiliated (AllCare, JCC, Columbia Pacific) | Not publicly specified — $1M/$3M recommended |
| Trillium | Not publicly specified — $1M/$3M recommended |
| EOCCO | Not publicly specified — $1M/$3M recommended |
| PacificSource (all service areas) | Not publicly specified — $1M/$3M recommended |

## What the Insurance Face Sheet Must Include

Your insurance face sheet (also called a certificate of insurance) is requested by nearly every CCO and must show:

- **Your full legal name** (matching your license and NPI)
- **Policy number**
- **Effective and expiration dates** (must be current)
- **Coverage limits** (per occurrence and aggregate amounts)
- **Insurance company name**

Keep a PDF copy ready — you'll upload it to CAQH ProView and attach it to multiple CCO applications.

## When to Get or Renew

- Get coverage **before** starting CAQH ProView setup (Step 8 requires uploading it)
- Set a renewal reminder 30 days before expiration
- When you renew, update your CAQH profile and notify any CCOs you're contracted with
