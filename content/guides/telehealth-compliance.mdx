# Telehealth Compliance for Oregon Medicaid

This guide covers billing and compliance requirements for delivering telehealth services to Oregon Medicaid (OHP) members. Rules apply across all CCOs, though individual CCOs may have additional requirements.

## Synchronous Video (Live Video)

Standard telehealth for behavioral health services:

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Modifier** | 95 (synchronous audio-video) |
| **Place of Service (POS)** | 02 (Telehealth — Other than Patient's Home) or 10 (Telehealth — Patient's Home) |
| **Platform** | HIPAA-compliant video platform |
| **Reimbursement** | Parity with in-person rates per ORS 743A.058 |

Use POS 10 when the patient is at their home. Use POS 02 for all other remote locations.

## Audio-Only (Telephone)

Oregon Medicaid covers audio-only behavioral health services under specific conditions:

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| **Modifier** | None (do not use modifier 95 for audio-only) |
| **Place of Service (POS)** | 02 |
| **Documentation** | Must document why video was not feasible or patient preference |
| **Reimbursement** | Parity with in-person rates |

Audio-only is permitted when video is not accessible to the patient or the patient prefers telephone. Document the reason in the clinical record.

## Reimbursement Parity

Per ORS 743A.058, Oregon requires telehealth reimbursement at the same rate as in-person services. This applies to both synchronous video and audio-only modalities. CCOs cannot pay less for a telehealth visit than the equivalent in-person service.

## Informed Consent

Best practice per OAR 309-114-0010:

- Inform the client about the nature of telehealth services
- Discuss limitations (technology failures, privacy risks)
- Obtain and document consent before the first telehealth session
- Review consent periodically (annually recommended)

While not all CCOs explicitly require written telehealth consent, maintaining a signed consent form protects both the practitioner and client.

## Platform Requirements

- Must be HIPAA-compliant (BAA with the platform vendor)
- Common compliant platforms: Doxy.me, Zoom for Healthcare, SimplePractice Telehealth
- Standard consumer platforms (FaceTime, Skype) are not HIPAA-compliant unless covered by a BAA

## CCO-Specific Notes

Most Oregon CCOs follow OHA's statewide telehealth policy. Verify with each CCO whether they have additional telehealth requirements or restrictions. Some CCOs may require you to indicate telehealth capability during credentialing.
