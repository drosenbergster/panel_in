# Mobile & Itinerant Practice Address Setup

If you provide services at multiple locations, in clients' homes, or through a mobile practice, you need to register your addresses correctly across all systems. Mismatched addresses are one of the most common reasons for enrollment delays.

## Register a Primary Business Address

Every provider enrollment system requires a primary business address. Even if you don't have a traditional office, you need one consistent address:

- **Home office** — acceptable if you conduct any administrative work there
- **Virtual office / mail service** — some systems accept this, but PECOS may not
- **Shared office space** — acceptable if you have a written agreement

This address must be consistent across NPPES, PECOS, OHA MMIS, and all CCO enrollments.

## OHP 3108: Mobile/Itinerant Questions

The OHP 3108 (Provider Enrollment Request for CCO Providers) asks whether you are a mobile or itinerant provider. If you provide services at multiple locations:

- Answer **"Yes"** to the mobile/itinerant question
- List your primary business address as the main practice location
- Note additional service locations in the supplemental section
- If you provide home-based services, indicate this clearly

## PECOS Address Requirements

Medicare (PECOS) has strict address requirements:

- Must be a physical address (not a P.O. Box)
- Must match your NPPES registration exactly
- If mobile/itinerant, register your primary administrative location
- PECOS allows you to add multiple practice locations after initial enrollment

## NPPES Address Matching

Your NPPES registration is the foundation that other systems verify against:

1. Log in to [NPPES](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov)
2. Update your **Practice Location Address** to your primary business address
3. Ensure this matches what you'll use on OHP 3108 and PECOS
4. If you have multiple locations, add them all in NPPES

## Common Mistakes

- Using a P.O. Box as your practice address in PECOS (rejected)
- Different addresses in NPPES vs. PECOS vs. CCO applications (causes delays)
- Not updating NPPES when you move (all downstream enrollments may be affected)
- Leaving the mobile/itinerant question blank on OHP 3108

## Checklist

Before submitting any enrollment applications, verify:

- [ ] Primary business address is consistent across all systems
- [ ] NPPES is updated with current practice location(s)
- [ ] OHP 3108 mobile/itinerant section is completed if applicable
- [ ] PECOS practice location matches NPPES exactly
