# OHA MMIS Provider Enrollment

**This is the statewide prerequisite.** No CCO will process your application without active enrollment in Oregon Health Authority's Division of Medical Assistance Programs (DMAP) through the MMIS system. Complete this step first before applying to any CCO.

## What You Need

- Active NPI (Type 1, individual) — registered in NPPES with correct name, address, and taxonomy
- Oregon LCSW license number
- Social Security Number
- Practice address

<TimeEstimate minutes={30} />

## Required Forms

| Form | Purpose | Download |
|------|---------|----------|
| **OHA 3972** | Individual provider enrollment application | [OHA Forms page](https://www.oregon.gov/oha/OHP/Providers/Pages/Forms.aspx) |
| **OHA 3975** | Provider enrollment agreement | [OHA Forms page](https://www.oregon.gov/oha/OHP/Providers/Pages/Forms.aspx) |

Both forms must be completed, signed, and submitted together.

## How to Enroll

1. Download OHA 3972 (individual application) and OHA 3975 (provider enrollment agreement) from the [OHA Forms page](https://www.oregon.gov/oha/OHP/Providers/Pages/Forms.aspx)
2. Complete both forms — your name and NPI must match your NPPES registry exactly
3. Submit via the [Oregon MMIS Provider Portal](https://www.or-medicaid.gov)
4. Wait for your **PIN letter** from OHA — this arrives within **5-6 business days**
5. Once you receive your PIN, you're registered with DMAP and can proceed to CCO applications

## Verify Your Enrollment Status

If you're unsure whether you're already enrolled, check using OHA's online tool:

- Go to the [OHA Provider Enrollment page](https://www.oregon.gov/oha/OHP/Providers/Pages/Enroll.aspx)
- Search by your NPI number
- If you're enrolled, you'll see your DMAP provider ID

## Key Requirements

- **NPI must match NPPES exactly**: Your name, address, and taxonomy code in NPPES must be identical to what you put on the OHA forms. Mismatches cause delays.
- **Keep your enrollment current**: Notify OHA of any address changes or license renewals.
- **Medicare too**: Even if you're only targeting Medicare (PECOS), OHA MMIS enrollment is still recommended as a foundation for any future Medicaid work.
