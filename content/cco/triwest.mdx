# TriWest Healthcare Alliance (TRICARE & VA Community Care)

TriWest Healthcare Alliance is a third-party administrator that manages two federal healthcare programs in the western United States. Credentialing with TriWest can give you access to both programs through a single organization.

**Two programs, one credentialing entity:**

- **TRICARE West Region** — Healthcare for active-duty military, retirees, and their families
- **VA Community Care Network (CCN)** — Healthcare for Veterans referred by the VA when VA facilities can't provide timely or nearby care

Both programs are federal and not tied to Oregon county boundaries — once credentialed, you can serve eligible beneficiaries anywhere in the West Region.

> **Verify availability first:** Before applying, confirm that TriWest is currently accepting new behavioral health providers in your area. Network availability changes based on demand. Call [877-226-8749](tel:877-226-8749) or email [providerservices@triwest.com](mailto:providerservices@triwest.com) to check.

## Before You Start

Confirm you have these ready:

- Active Oregon LCSW license (current, unexpired)
- NPI (Type 1, individual) — registered in NPPES
- Professional liability (malpractice) insurance meeting Oregon state minimums
- Current CV/resume with month and year for all dates (gaps over 6 months must be explained)
- DEA certificate (if applicable — not required for most LCSWs)
- CAQH ProView profile (optional but strongly recommended — simplifies the application)

## Step 1: Check Network Availability

Contact TriWest to confirm they are accepting new behavioral health providers in your area before investing time in the application.

- **Phone:** [877-226-8749](tel:877-226-8749) (877-CCN-TRIW)
- **Email:** [providerservices@triwest.com](mailto:providerservices@triwest.com)
- **Web:** [triwest.com/provider](https://www.triwest.com/provider)

Ask specifically: *"Are you currently accepting new LCSW/behavioral health providers in Oregon for TRICARE and/or VA Community Care?"*

<TimeEstimate minutes={15} />

## Step 2: Submit the Network Application

Go to [joinournetwork.triwest.com](https://joinournetwork.triwest.com) and complete the provider enrollment form.

**If you have a CAQH profile:** You can submit just page 2 of the application along with your demographic information and CAQH ID number — TriWest will pull the rest from CAQH. This is significantly faster than the full packet.

**If you don't have a CAQH profile**, you'll need to submit the full credentialing packet including:

| Document | Details |
|----------|---------|
| State license | Copy of current unexpired Oregon LCSW license |
| Malpractice insurance | Current declaration sheet showing coverage limits and expiration |
| CV/Resume | Month and year for all dates; explain any gaps over 6 months |
| Board certification | Copy if applicable |
| NPI | Individual NPI number |
| DEA certificate | Copy if applicable |

**Submit to:** [credentialing@triwest.com](mailto:credentialing@triwest.com) or through the online portal

<TimeEstimate minutes={45} />

## Step 3: Credentialing Verification

After submission, TriWest performs primary-source verification of your:

- Education and training
- Board certification (if applicable)
- State licensure
- Professional background and work history
- Malpractice claims history
- CMS exclusions/sanctions list check

You do not need to do anything during this step — TriWest handles verification directly. You'll be notified if additional documentation is needed.

**Disqualifiers:** Providers on the CMS exclusions/sanctions list cannot participate in TriWest networks.

<TimeEstimate minutes={5} />

## Step 4: Contract Execution

Once credentialing is approved, TriWest (or a local network representative) will send you a provider contract. Review and sign the contract, which requires compliance with:

- All TRICARE rules and requirements (for TRICARE network)
- VA Community Care Network policies (for CCN)
- TriWest policies and procedures
- Applicable federal and state regulations

You are considered an active network provider once you receive notification of final contract execution.

<TimeEstimate minutes={30} />

## Step 5: Complete Required Training (VA CCN)

If you are joining the VA Community Care Network, complete TriWest's required and recommended training:

- **CCN Provider Training** — Available through the Availity portal after contracting
- **Veterans Crisis Line Protocol** — Know how to connect Veterans to the Veterans Crisis Line (988, press 1)
- **Evidence-Based Psychotherapy** — Familiarity with VA-recommended treatment approaches for PTSD, depression, and substance use disorders
- **HSRM (HealthShare Referral Manager)** — Learn how VA referrals are routed and managed

Access training materials at [vaccn.triwest.com](https://vaccn.triwest.com) after receiving your provider login.

<TimeEstimate minutes={60} />

## TRICARE vs. VA Community Care: Key Differences

| | TRICARE West | VA Community Care (CCN) |
|---|---|---|
| **Who it serves** | Active-duty military, retirees, and dependents | Veterans referred by VA |
| **How patients find you** | Beneficiaries search the TRICARE provider directory | VA issues a referral through HSRM |
| **Referral required?** | No — direct access for most behavioral health services | Yes — Veterans must have a VA-issued referral |
| **Authorization** | Most outpatient BH services don't require prior auth | Authorization managed through the VA referral |
| **Claims portal** | Availity | Availity |

## Re-credentialing

TriWest re-credentials providers **every 3 years**. Your approval continues upon re-credentialing unless otherwise notified. Set a reminder for your re-credentialing date when you receive your initial approval.

## Oregon-Specific Notes

- **TRICARE West Region:** Oregon is part of the TRICARE West Region managed by TriWest (covering 13 western states)
- **VA CCN Region:** Oregon falls under TriWest's VA Community Care territory
- **Telehealth:** Both TRICARE and VA CCN support telehealth services — verify specific telehealth policies through the Availity provider portal
- **Supervision:** LCSWs are recognized as "Certified Clinical Social Workers" under TRICARE and can practice independently (no physician supervision required for most outpatient behavioral health services)
- **Military cultural competency:** While not a formal requirement, familiarity with military culture, deployment-related stress, and military sexual trauma is strongly valued by both programs
