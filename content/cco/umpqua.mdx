# Umpqua Health Alliance Provider Enrollment

Umpqua Health Alliance serves Douglas County. Umpqua uses a **two-stage credentialing process** — an initial questionnaire followed by a full application. Providers **cannot see members until credentialing is fully complete**.

## Critical: No Billing Before Credentialing

Unlike some CCOs, Umpqua Health Alliance does not permit providers to see members or bill for services until the full credentialing process is complete and a contract is executed. Plan accordingly.

## Before You Start

Confirm you have these ready:

- Active DMAP registration (OHA MMIS enrollment complete)
- Active Oregon LCSW license
- NPI (Type 1, individual)
- CAQH ProView profile (completed and attested)
- Professional liability insurance — **minimum $1M/$3M required**
- Three professional peer references (names and contact info)

## Stage 1: Provider Request Questionnaire

Contact Umpqua Health Alliance to request the **Provider Request Questionnaire**. This initial screening form collects basic practice information and determines whether Umpqua has a network need in your area and specialty.

**Website:** [umpquahealth.com/providers/partner-with-us/credentialing](https://www.umpquahealth.com/providers/partner-with-us/credentialing/)

<TimeEstimate minutes={20} />

## Stage 2: Full Credentialing Application

If your questionnaire is accepted, Umpqua will send the full credentialing application. This requires:

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Completed credentialing application | Provided by Umpqua after Stage 1 approval |
| Three professional peer references | Must be from licensed professionals familiar with your clinical work |
| Professional liability insurance face sheet | **Minimum $1M/$3M** — Umpqua is strict on this requirement |
| Copy of active Oregon LCSW license | Current, unencumbered |
| Work history | Complete employment history, gaps explained |
| Education verification | Degree and program details |

<TimeEstimate minutes={60} />

## Timeline

Umpqua's credentialing process takes **up to 120 days** — longer than the standard 90-day ORS 743B.454 timeline for most CCOs. The two-stage process adds time because the questionnaire must be reviewed before the full application is issued.

| Stage | Expected Duration |
|-------|------------------|
| Stage 1: Questionnaire review | 2–4 weeks |
| Stage 2: Full credentialing | Up to 90 days after application submitted |
| **Total** | **Up to 120 days** |

## After Credentialing

Once fully credentialed, Umpqua will issue a provider contract. You may begin seeing Umpqua members only after the contract is executed.
