# InterCommunity Health Network (IHN-CCO) Provider Enrollment

IHN-CCO (InterCommunity Health Network) serves Benton, Lincoln, and Linn counties. IHN-CCO is operated by Samaritan Health Plans and uses a straightforward enrollment process.

## Before You Start

Confirm you have these ready:

- Active DAMP registration (OHA MMIS enrollment complete)
- Active Oregon LCSW license
- NPI (Type 1, individual)
- CAQH ProView profile (completed and attested)
- Professional liability insurance ($1M/$3M)

## Step 1: Complete the OHP 3108

Download and complete the [OHP 3108 (Provider Enrollment Request for CCO Providers)](https://www.oregon.gov/oha/HSD/OHP/Tools/Provider%20Enrollment%20Request%20for%20CCO%20Providers%20OHP%203108%201.2025.pdf). Specify **InterCommunity Health Network** as the CCO.

<TimeEstimate minutes={15} />

## Step 2: Gather Required Documents

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| OHP 3108 | Specify IHN-CCO |
| Professional liability insurance face sheet | Current, showing $1M/$3M coverage |
| Copy of active Oregon LCSW license | Current, unencumbered |
| W-9 form | Current, signed |

<TimeEstimate minutes={15} />

## Step 3: Contact IHN-CCO

**Phone:** (541) 768-4550

**Website:** [ihntogether.org/for-providers](https://ihntogether.org/for-providers)

Call to confirm current enrollment requirements and submit your documents. IHN-CCO's provider services team can confirm network availability in your area.

## No Prior Authorization Required

IHN-CCO does **not** require prior authorization for routine outpatient therapy services. This makes it one of the more straightforward CCOs for behavioral health providers.

## Timeline

Standard credentialing timeline is **up to 90 days** per ORS 743B.454.
