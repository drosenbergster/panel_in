# Cascade Health Alliance Provider Enrollment

Cascade Health Alliance serves Klamath County. Cascade is a smaller CCO and provider enrollment details should be confirmed directly with their office.

## Before You Start

Confirm you have these ready:

- Active DMAP registration (OHA MMIS enrollment complete)
- Active Oregon LCSW license
- NPI (Type 1, individual)
- CAQH ProView profile (completed and attested)
- Professional liability insurance ($1M/$3M)

## Step 1: Contact Cascade Health Alliance

Contact Cascade's provider services to request current enrollment materials and confirm network availability:

**Website:** [cascadehealthalliance.com/for-providers](https://www.cascadehealthalliance.com/for-providers/)

<TimeEstimate minutes={10} />

## Step 2: Complete Required Forms

Based on your initial contact, Cascade will provide their specific enrollment forms. Standard requirements typically include:

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| OHP 3108 or CCO-specific enrollment form | As directed by Cascade |
| Professional liability insurance face sheet | Current, showing $1M/$3M coverage |
| Copy of active Oregon LCSW license | Current, unencumbered |
| W-9 form | Current, signed |

<TimeEstimate minutes={30} />

## Step 3: Submit and Follow Up

Submit your completed packet as directed by Cascade's provider services team.

## Klamath County Overlap

Klamath County is also served by [PacificSource Central Oregon](/pathway/pacificsource-central-oregon). If you selected Klamath County, both Cascade and PacificSource will appear in your pathway. These are separate CCOs requiring separate credentialing.

## Timeline

Standard credentialing timeline is **up to 90 days** per ORS 743B.454.
