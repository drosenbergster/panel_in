# Medicare Provider Enrollment (PECOS)

Medicare enrollment is a federal process separate from Oregon CCO credentialing. You enroll through the **PECOS** (Provider Enrollment, Chain, and Ownership System) portal. Once enrolled, you can bill Medicare directly for eligible patients regardless of which Oregon counties you serve.

## Before You Start

Confirm you have these ready:

- Active Oregon LCSW license
- NPI (Type 1, individual) — registered in NPPES
- Social Security Number and date of birth
- Practice location address (must match NPPES)
- Professional liability insurance (active policy)
- Bank account information for Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT)

## Step 1: Access the PECOS Portal

Go to [pecos.cms.hhs.gov](https://pecos.cms.hhs.gov) and log in with your EIDM (Enterprise Identity Management) account. If you don't have an EIDM account, you'll need to create one first at [nppes.cms.hhs.gov](https://nppes.cms.hhs.gov).

<TimeEstimate minutes={15} />

## Step 2: Start a New Enrollment Application

Select **"Enroll as a New Provider"** and choose the appropriate application type:

- **Application Type:** Individual practitioner (CMS-855I)
- **Specialty:** Clinical Social Worker (taxonomy code 104100000X)
- **State:** Oregon
- **MAC (Medicare Administrative Contractor):** Noridian Healthcare Solutions (Oregon's MAC)

<TimeEstimate minutes={10} />

## Step 3: Complete the CMS-855I Application

The online application collects the following information:

| Section | Required Information |
|---------|---------------------|
| Personal | Legal name, SSN, date of birth |
| Licensure | Oregon LCSW license number, issue/expiration dates |
| NPI | Your individual NPI number |
| Practice location | Physical address (must match NPPES exactly) |
| Mailing address | Where Medicare correspondence should be sent |
| Specialty | Clinical Social Worker (104100000X) |
| Billing | Group or individual billing, EFT information |

**Address matching is critical:** Your practice address in PECOS must exactly match what's registered in NPPES. Mismatches will delay processing.

<TimeEstimate minutes={60} />

## Step 4: Review and Submit

Review all sections carefully before submission. Common rejection reasons:

- NPI not active or not matching NPPES records
- Practice address mismatch between PECOS and NPPES
- Missing or expired liability insurance
- Incorrect taxonomy code

After submission, print or save your confirmation page for your records.

## Step 5: Complete EFT Setup

If not done during the application, set up Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT) through PECOS. Medicare payments require direct deposit.

<TimeEstimate minutes={15} />

## Timeline

| Milestone | Expected Duration |
|-----------|------------------|
| EIDM account creation | Same day |
| Application review by Noridian | 30–60 days |
| Medicare effective date | Typically the date the application was filed |
| PTAN (Provider Transaction Access Number) issuance | With approval letter |

## After Enrollment: 5-Year Revalidation

Medicare requires **revalidation every 5 years**. CMS will notify you approximately 6 months before your revalidation due date. Failure to revalidate on time results in deactivation of your Medicare billing privileges.

Set a reminder for your revalidation date when you receive your initial approval.

## Oregon-Specific Notes

- **MAC:** Noridian Healthcare Solutions processes Medicare claims for Oregon
- **Telehealth:** Medicare telehealth rules differ from Medicaid — verify covered services and originating site requirements
- **Supervision:** Clinical Social Workers can bill Medicare independently (no physician supervision required)
