# Oregon Medicaid Reimbursement Rates

This reference summarizes how Oregon Medicaid reimbursement works for behavioral health providers. Rates vary by CCO, service type, and geographic designation.

## OHA Fee-for-Service (FFS) Base Rates

The Oregon Health Authority publishes Fee-for-Service rates that serve as the **reference floor** for Medicaid reimbursement. These are the minimum rates paid for services delivered outside of a CCO contract.

Current FFS fee schedules are available at the [OHA Fee Schedule page](https://www.oregon.gov/oha/HSD/OHP/Pages/Fee-Schedule.aspx).

Common behavioral health CPT codes for outpatient therapy:

| CPT Code | Description | Approximate FFS Rate |
|----------|-------------|---------------------|
| 90834 | Individual therapy, 45 min | Verify current schedule |
| 90837 | Individual therapy, 60 min | Verify current schedule |
| 90847 | Family therapy with patient | Verify current schedule |
| 90853 | Group therapy | Verify current schedule |

**Note:** FFS rates change periodically. Always verify current rates on the OHA website.

## Directed Payment Tiers

Oregon's Directed Payment program provides enhanced rates above the FFS base:

| Tier | Rate Enhancement | Applies To |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| **Rural** | +27% above FFS | Providers in rural-designated areas |
| **Non-rural** | +22% above FFS | Providers in non-rural areas |

Geographic designation is based on your practice location, not the patient's location. Rural designations follow OHA's published list of rural ZIP codes.

## CCO Contract Rates

Individual CCO contract rates **differ from FFS rates** and are negotiated between the CCO and the provider (or provider organization). Key points:

- CCO rates may be higher or lower than FFS + directed payment
- Rates are specified in your provider contract with each CCO
- Some CCOs publish fee schedules; others provide them during contracting
- You can negotiate rates during the contracting process

## Key Takeaways

- FFS rates are the floor, not the ceiling
- Directed payments add 22–27% depending on rural/non-rural designation
- Your actual reimbursement depends on your CCO contract
- You can and should negotiate rates during contracting
